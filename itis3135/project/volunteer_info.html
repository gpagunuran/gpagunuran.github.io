Volunteer Opportunities

Thank you for your interest in volunteering with Siembra NC! Our volunteers are the backbone of our organization, helping us create meaningful change in our communities. Below you'll find information about what volunteering entails and how you can get involved.

What Volunteers Do

As a Siembra NC volunteer, you'll participate in activities that support community organizing and civic engagement. Volunteer opportunities include:

  • Community Outreach: Connect with community members, share information about important issues, and encourage civic participation
  • Event Support: Help organize and staff community meetings, workshops, and events
  • Phone Banking: Make calls to community members to share information and gather input on local issues
  • Canvassing: Go door-to-door in neighborhoods to engage with residents about community concerns
  • Administrative Support: Assist with data entry, social media, and other organizational tasks

Time Commitment

We understand that everyone has different schedules and availability. Whether you can volunteer for a few hours once a month or multiple times per week, we appreciate your contribution. Most volunteer shifts are 2-4 hours long and can be scheduled at your convenience.

We offer flexible scheduling with shifts available during weekdays, evenings, and weekends. You can choose the activities and times that work best for you.

Training and Support

All volunteers receive training before their first shift. We'll provide you with:

  • An orientation session covering our mission, values, and approach
  • Specific training for your volunteer role
  • All necessary materials and resources
  • Ongoing support from our staff and experienced volunteers
  • Opportunities to develop new skills and meet like-minded people

Requirements

To volunteer with Siembra NC, you should:

  • Be at least 16 years old (younger volunteers welcome with parent/guardian participation)
  • Be committed to our values of community empowerment and social justice
  • Be reliable and communicate any schedule changes in advance
  • Treat all community members with respect and dignity

What to Expect on Your First Day

When you arrive for your first volunteer shift:

  • Check in with the volunteer coordinator or designated staff member
  • Receive any materials you'll need for your shift
  • Get paired with an experienced volunteer or staff member for guidance
  • Ask questions - we're here to help you feel comfortable and confident

Please dress comfortably and appropriately for the activity. We recommend comfortable shoes, weather-appropriate clothing, and bringing a water bottle.
