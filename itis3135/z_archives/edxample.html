Esports

This page is going to explore one of my hobbies and interests: Esports!

I would define esports as any type of competitive video game organized and officiated by an authority figure. This figure can be a sponsoring company, school, or just a game's server host. My go-to esports video games are CS2, Valorant, and other FPS games. My hobby includes not only playing in esports, but working in esports events. I am interested in the high school, collegiate, and international esports scenes.

Who

The goal of the esports industry is to professionalize and grow competitive gaming by creating sustainable ecosystems that support players, teams, organizers and fans—fostering fair competition, player development and welfare—while generating revenue through sponsorships, media rights, merchandise and live events to expand the global audience and mainstream recognition of esports.

Some of my favorite teams/orgs:

  • Ninjas in Pyjamas
  • FaZe Clan (I worked for them!)
  • Paper Rex
  • Fnatic

When

The esports events that I follow usually culminate in the fall season. VCT Champions, Counter-Strike Majors.

This usually means that fall is my busiest season when it comes to working at these events, but you can also sign up to work at any nerdy convention, which is bound to have a competitive scene in some game. I personally don't like working at fighting game events, but some of my friends play such games, so I go watch them play sometimes.

Where

The esports industry has given me the opportunity to travel to many different places in America! My next goal is to visit the CS2 Cologne Majors in Germany.

Working at esports events don't usually pay much, but I'm provided free admission into the event, free meals, I get to be in contact with the pros, and get reimbursed for room and board when travelling. I'd say that brings more than enough value for me to justify the pay rate.

Why

Let me think about why I love esports so much...

Because it's fun!

I can't imagine being an esports pro, spending every day practicing to become the best in your respective game, being paid to play video games well! I like to live vicariously through watching them play, and I have more fun playing these games afterwards as well. Also, I am able to use my skills as a student in computer science to quickly troubleshoot and fix issues with PCs and other systems when working in these events. I would love if I could continue to work in this industry, but in a higher level once I get my degree.

How

I've wanted to be involved in esports since I first watched CS:GO majors in 2016. I have my friends in UNC Charlotte to thank for all these connections I've made within the esports industry. Niner Esports has given me a jumpstart to (hopefully) a future career working with video games and competitive play.
